Plant count

 

The first fast growing trees like the flowering ash planted by the Association ten years ago , are now over 2 meters tall.  Some have got to more than 3 meters – like the one on the photo.
 

They produce seeds which when disseminated by the wind engender fresh seedlings producing what will then spread into a  whole forest in a  perfectly natural way.

Precise statistics on the success rate of planting were drawn up by a student who used the findings in his agricultural thesis at the University of Provence. He worked on a representative site reforested by the association ten years ago.  A full count of the existing or missing trees was made along with other parameters , followed by a concluding synthesis.

The count carried out in Spring 2001 concerned 2719 plots out of which 2509 are now occupied by a living tree.  The success rate after ten years is therefore 91.6%  which indisputably proves the validity of the planting methods used and the choice of species.

Indeed, this particular reforestation comprises 70% ash, 20% oak and 10% true service trees.
The initial number of each species planted is unknown so it is impossible to calculate the actual success rate per species. What can be said, however, is that the minimal success rate is 87% for ash, 67% for oak and 53% for the service trees.  The success rate for the oaks which will become the dominant tree of future forests is, in itself, already very satisfactory.
