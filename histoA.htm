Background and objectives of the association:

The Association for the Replanting and Protection of the Cengle Sainte-Victoire (A.R.P.C.V.) was created in 1986 as an answer to the ravages left by the disastrous fire which spread across the Cengle, the plateau situated at the southern foot of the mountain, Sainte-Victoire, close to Aix en Provence. The association's immediate objective at the time was to repair the damage by clearing up the area and planting new trees on this part of the mountain.

The idea defended by the association, was to break the natural cycle pine forest – fire, and to plant broad-leaved trees, more able to resist fires, and very close together, so as to prevent the growth of the garrigue which propagates the fire.
 

Up to now, around 100000 trees have been planted by the association
on Sainte Victoire and the Cengle.

In 1989, after the second terrible forest fire on Sainte-Victoire itself, the association's action and goals were reinforced and its importance has been growing steadily ever since. Thanks to the success of our tree planting programs and an increasing demand from schools for environmental education, we have extended our activities on making young people aware of the natural riches the mountain offers and the need to protect them.
