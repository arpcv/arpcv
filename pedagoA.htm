ENVIRONMENTAL  EDUCATION



Public footpaths, and guided tours organised by the ARPCV

So as to help people better know the nature surrounding them, the association has  created two public footpaths at the foot of Sainte Victoire :

- A « botanic footpath ».
It includes around hundred trees or shrubs that one comes across in Provence , with a board giving its name. It is access free, but half  day visits accompanied by a guide from the ARPCV can be organised for adults or children.

- A  « discovery footpath »

This allows for greater awareness of the local ecology  and history of the mountain ( trees, flowers, animals, as well as geology, archaeology, and traditions…).
Visits with a guide from the  ARPCV are organised.
A book describing this footpath has been edited by the association (Edisud- 1998)

If you are interested in one of these visits, contact the ARPCV.
 
 

Educational activities

The association now  plays a major role  in environmental education for children. Every year about 1000 pupils go round the two circuits created by the association, and about 600 are invited to the special events organised by the association.
 

*   Regular activities
These  are proposed for  schools and kids leisure centers. Their aim is to make the young into eco-citizens, more aware of the need for nature protection, and subjects like nature, the forest, water, waste  … are illustrated and discussed.
Other  subjects can be dealt with according to teachers’ demands

*  Special events :  The Nature Festival  (beginning of June)

600 pupils are invited together at the foot of Sainte Victoire, where 20 stands and activities are proposed, to discover nature and to learn about environmental protection
The subjects are for instance:   how to read a pedestrian map, how dinosaur’s eggs are collected, , waste selection at home, the advantages of gas heating …

This organization received a special prize from the Ministry of the Environment in July 2001, which demonstrates the quality of the  ARPCV’s activity for the environment
