Site maintenance 

 
 

The trees planted and protected in this way are then left to themselves.  After a few years the scrubland which has grown again and which, at first, offered them some extra natural protection, begins to hamper  their growth and above all presents a real fire risk.   Regular maintenance of the site is therefore imperative.

Volunteer members of the Association ensure this upkeep once a month.

 * The plastic protection is taken off when the plant is about 1 meter high (after three or four years) and can resist drought.
 * The highly inflammable shrubs (argeiras, juniper bushes etc.) are cut down with motor ground clearers
* The holly oaks which spreads naturally is cleared or cut back so as to improve its growth and give a somewhat more natural, wild aspect to the planted areas.
For the same reasons a few pine trees are left to break up the landscape.

 
    These friendly, fun days are very satisfying as the volunteer members can ,over the years, see the fruit of their efforts as the trees grow.
