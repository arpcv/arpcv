The choice of species 

(The photos were taken one year after the tree planting day)



The trees chosen are native to the region ,adapted to its ecology, able to throw out new shoots from a stump, deciduous, with leaves which decompose easily (good for the formation of biologically active humus).  Planted in mixed groupings, they offer a high fire-resistance factor.
 

  The flowering ash (Fraxinus ornus), has been chosen as the pioneer colonising species since it develops quickly even on poor soil. Seeding is through wind dispersion thus ensuring rapid spread and protection of other species of slower growth , which explains why at least half the trees planted should be the flowering ash.  It lives for almost fifty years and can grow to 15 meters high.

     The intermediary heliophile  species chosen for its rich fruit dispersed by animals is the true service tree (Sorbus domestica).  Slower growing than the ash it can grow to 20 meters tall and live from one to two centuries.
 

  The species with the longest length of life is the downy oak ( Quercus pubescens).  A slow growing tree demanding lateral shade , it is common in the lower mountain regions of the Mediterranean and can reach 25 meters.  It lives several centuries and the ground beneath and surrounding it can often produce truffles.

A few extra species such as the white-beam or Montpellier maple are added.  On the northern slopes or on well watered ground some less hardy species are used.
