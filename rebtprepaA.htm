Ground preparation 


 

* The ground is cleared of any burnt trees and the often all-invasive dense prickly vegetation in order to make the volunteers’ work simpler.

 

                                                                   

* The land is tilled by a powerful tractor which prepares the earth in long, parallel grooves 20cm wide and 80 cm deep, 1.50m apart, along which the trees are planted.

 

                                                                         

* This stage is essential as it ensures the penetration of the young roots as well as of any rain.  It also facilitates the work of the volunteers who have to dig the holes for planting !

These first two stages are left to professionals.

* The next stage consists of pegging out the ground with wooden stakes to mark the place where each tree is to be planted.  This is the work of association's members the day before the annual tree planting.  Since the aim is to obtain a thick covering which will impede the growth of inflammable undergrowth, planting is dense:  one tree every 1.5 m in staggered rows.

                                                                       

The ground is now ready for the volunteer planters.
