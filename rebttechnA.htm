Planting methods

 



* The main objective is to obtain as quickly as possible a dense cover which will eliminate the highly inflammable sun loving undergrowth.  In time, this latter will quite naturally be progressively replaced by other species more adapted to the conditions of reduced light.

* Planting is therefore dense  -  2000 trees per acre which represents one tree every 1.5 meters.  If the ground is flat and the soil deep enough, the land is ploughed in order to get rid of all unwanted vegetation and improve the chances of water retention as well as ease the digging of holes.

* The plants bought at the nursery measure around 20 cm in small earthpacks which ensure their survival for a short time after replanting.

Three sprouting acorns are placed directly in the soil to ensure oak growth.   A light covering of earth protects them against predators.

* As an aid to rooting and early growth, a transparent plastic covering which acts as a greenhouse and a protection against wind and drought, weeds and animals , is placed over each plant.  It also ensures vertical growth.
